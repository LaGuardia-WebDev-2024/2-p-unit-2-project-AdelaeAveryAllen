dyslexia

What is it?

Let's start with a definition; dyslexia is a a neurobiological learning disability that results in difficulties with language processing, particularly reading, spelling, and writing. This is a life long disability and is caused by differences in how the brain develpos. It is also gentic and runs in familys.

What does it look like how does it affect people?

Some symptoms include trouble with decoding (sounding out words), encoding (spelling words), and fluency (reading at a good pace and without mistakes) As well as difficutly processing infomation, sequences, and more. Finally, other common signs are not understanding what you’ve read, making a lot of mistakes when reading, quickly forgetting names of people and places, feeling tired after reading, avoiding reading, and difficulty with spelling and writing.
